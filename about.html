I did my undergraduation in Computer Science. I’m the cliché example of a kid who spent his initial university years studying something he never enjoyed or felt destined to do. When I was introduced to Web Development, I fell in love with it. Finally, I found something which I'd love to do. I realized how it enables us to solve non-trivial problems.

For ex: Can you imagine your life without Google Maps? No, right? For some, it may be just another app; but for me (for you as well, if you're here), it's a masterpiece. It tries to solve very simple (which ain't that simple) problem - it helps you navigate from one place to other. But, there are so many things going on BTS. Similarly, I love to use my skills to solve real world problems, by developing applications which are user friendly and which make users' lives easier!

Fast forward to today, I completed my Master's in Computer Science from Rutgers University, New Brunswick in December'21. After that I joined Google as Full Time Software Engineer in their Seattle office. During my journey, I have worked with BCG, RBC, Accenture as SWE. Over the past 3 years I've learnt a lot as a Software Engineer. I have realized that techonology changes every day as every other industry & as we go more digital, world's requirements keep on changing frequently. So I never stop learning about latest technology, new languages, DS & A and how these merge together. I think learning is a mindset - if I stop, I’ll be out of business in a few months... or years maybe.

I always look around to find the problem I can solve by technology and then I work on that. To find out the projects I've worked on, visit Projects page.
